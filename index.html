About Lebanon

Location of Lebanon

The State of Lebanon is located in the north-west of the Levant, to the north of Palestine, and to the west of Syria, and overlooking the waters of the Mediterranean Sea from its western side. This unique geographical location has helped it to possess many characteristics and elements that no other country can afford.

Nature in Lebanon

The State of Lebanon is distinguished by its diverse terrain and features, which has provided unparalleled beauty. It can be said that the western region of Lebanon is a coastal area, the Lebanese coast is known for its beauty, and that many of the important Lebanese cities overlook it, especially the city of Beirut, the capital of the country. Lebanon contains the famous Bekaa Valley; it is one of the most fertile areas in Lebanon. It also contains two of the most important rivers: the Asi River and the Litani River. Lebanon has a distinctive type of trees, namely, cedar trees. This tree has become a symbol of the country’s fame, Lebanese territory contains large areas of forests where these trees are located.

The most beautiful Lebanese monuments

There are many landmarks in Lebanon that reflect the beauty of this land, and its ability to be at the top of the list of the most beautiful countries not only in the Arab region, but perhaps in the whole world as well.One of these features is the magnificent cave known as the Jeita Grotto, which contains many cavities, as well as many exotic and beautiful forms at the same time. Also the beautiful resorts are among the most prominent of the Lebanese landmarks, and it includes many slopes which provide a suitable place for those wishing to ski. It is also possible to say that Beirut is one of the most beautiful areas in the country, because of its history of originality and beauty.Lebanon also contains a number of important nature reserves, created to preserve the unique nature of the country.The most prominent of these reserves are: Al-Shouf Cedar Reserve, Palm Islands Reserve, Harsh Ehden Reserve, Afqa Natural Reserve, and many other areas. Each of these reserves has different characteristics than other reserves.
